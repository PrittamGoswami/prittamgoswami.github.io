Academic Projects

Detection and Investigation of De novo mutations

Oct 2022–Present

Supervisor: Prof Kenta Nakai, The Institute of Medical Sciences, The University of Tokyo, Tokyo

The project can be divided into two parts. In the first part of the project I am developing a rapid algorithm for mining point de novo mutations across multiple genomes of a species. In the second step, I plan to use this algorithm for creating a mutation dataset and then investigating it for finding patterns behind the occurence of similar types of mutations.

A new approach to subunit vaccines using genetically engineered bacteriophages, that combines the safety of subunit vaccines with the long-lasting immunogenicity of live-attenuated vaccines

Mar 2020–Aug 2020

My hypothesis proposes to exploit the human microbiome by infecting them with genetically-modified bacteria capable of expressing the immunogenic protein, leading to the subsequent expression and absorption of the immunogen into our body, thus, facilitating a safe and long-lasting immune response.

Feedback Thermal Cycler-A machine for the bulk production of DNA templates to boost up cell-free protein synthesis

Dec 2017–Present

I have theoretically developed the design of a specialized thermal cycler than can work continuously and recursively to produce a large amount of DNA templates that can be used for carrying out cell-free protein synthesis in large scale.
